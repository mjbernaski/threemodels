Yes, absolutely. Minnesota, particularly the Minneapolis-St. Paul metro area, has a robust and growing ecosystem of companies that are either AI-focused or leverage AI and machine learning as a core part of their business.

The state's AI scene is characterized by its strength inapplied AI, where technology is used to solve real-world problems in major industries like healthcare, retail, agriculture, and manufacturing.

1. Large Enterprises with Major AI/Data Science Teams

Many of Minnesota's Fortune 500 companies have invested heavily in AI, making them some of the largest employers of data scientists and machine learning engineers in the state.

  • Optum / UnitedHealth Group (Minnetonka):A global leader in healthcare. Optum's AI teams work on everything from predictive analytics for patient outcomes and fraud detection to natural language processing (NLP) for clinical notes and optimizing claims processing.
  • Target (Minneapolis):The retail giant uses AI and ML extensively for personalized recommendations, supply chain optimization, demand forecasting, computer vision in stores, and marketing analytics.
  • Best Buy (Richfield):Similar to Target, they use AI for e-commerce personalization, customer service chatbots, supply chain logistics, and understanding customer behavior.
  • 3M (Maplewood):A diversified technology company that applies AI in materials science research, manufacturing process optimization (industrial IoT), computer vision for quality control, and NLP for analyzing scientific papers.
  • Cargill (Wayzata):As a global food and agriculture corporation, Cargill uses AI for precision agriculture, optimizing commodity trading, monitoring crop health via satellite imagery, and improving supply chain efficiency.
  • U.S. Bank (Minneapolis):A major financial institution using AI for fraud detection, credit risk assessment, algorithmic trading, and personalized digital banking experiences.

2. Established Tech Companies & "Unicorns"

These are pure-play technology companies headquartered in Minnesota that have AI/ML at their core.

  • Jamf (Minneapolis):A leader in Apple device management. Jamf uses machine learning for security threat detection and IT automation on Apple devices.
  • Code42 (Minneapolis):A cybersecurity company focused on insider risk management. Their platform uses machine learning to detect unusual employee file activity that might signal a data leak or theft.
  • SPS Commerce (Minneapolis):A cloud-based supply chain management company. They use AI to help retailers and suppliers manage their data, providing analytics and insights to improve efficiency.
  • Calabrio (Minneapolis):Specializes in workforce engagement management for contact centers. They use AI for speech analytics, sentiment analysis on customer calls, and predictive analytics for call volumes.
  • Total Expert (St. Louis Park):A marketing and customer engagement platform for the financial services industry (mortgage, banking). They use AI to automate marketing and predict which customers are most likely to need a new loan or service.

3. Emerging Startups & Smaller Firms

  • Inspectorio (Minneapolis):Provides an AI-powered platform for quality control and supply chain visibility, using computer vision and data analytics to inspect products and facilities remotely.
  • Voxel (Minneapolis office):Uses computer vision and AI to analyze security camera footage in real-time to identify and prevent workplace accidents in warehouses and manufacturing plants.
  • Branch (Minneapolis):While known for mobile linking, their platform uses sophisticated data models and machine learning to attribute user actions across different platforms.
  • Dispatch (Bloomington):An on-demand delivery platform that uses AI for route optimization and logistics management.
  • MentorMate (Minneapolis):A prominent software and data science consulting firm that builds custom AI and machine learning solutions for other businesses.

  • Community & Meetups:Groups likeAI-MNand other tech organizations host regular events, fostering a sense of community and knowledge sharing.
