Toni Morrison was a woman and one of the most celebrated American authors of the 20th and early 21st centuries. She was born Chloe Ardelia Wofford in 1931 and passed away in 2019.

Morrison was renowned for her powerful novels exploring African American experiences, particularly focusing on the lives of Black women. Her most famous works include "Beloved," "Song of Solomon," "The Bluest Eye," and "Jazz."

She achieved numerous prestigious honors, including:

  • The Nobel Prize in Literature (1993) - she was the first African American woman to receive this award
  • The Pulitzer Prize for Fiction (1988) for "Beloved"
